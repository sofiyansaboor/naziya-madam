 3. Certification in Tally

Tally is the most common course that is offered to every student in the commerce stream. Although, it is the robust accounting system used by small and medium-sized businesses for doing the calculations.

Chartered accountancy is a profession that manages financial accounts of companies and businesses following the laws set by the government. If you want to become CA then, you need to pursue the Chartered Accountant professional course and you may be a member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India. 

Want to enroll for courses after 12th for commerce to get high salary?

Well, to be a member of ICAI you need to attend the CPT exam. You can appear for the exam after completing your 12th. As you need to clear the CPT exam to appear for C.A final exam.

Lastly, you will be in the position of Chartered Accountant. Although, studying for C.A is tough but the career prospects are exciting.

11. Certified Management Accountant (CMA)

The certified management accountant course is offered by The Institute of Cost and Work Accountants of India (ICWAI). The earliest, ICWAI used to offer a Cost and Works Accountancy (CWA) course. The CMA course is a combined element of CS and CA. 

As it holds few common subjects of CS and CA course stream and it has three level of course as starts with Foundation, Intermediate, and followed by Final. 

All 12th Standard passed students need to start with Foundation level and start the CMA course. The Foundation level will be for one year and the same is for the other two-level as well. 

Let’s have a look over the eligibility and the syllabus of the CMA course. Here we go. 

 

Eligibility and Syllabus of the Course

You need to score a minimum of 60% percentage to start the CMA course. Now, the syllabus of the course is structured below respectively with the level. 

 

Foundation Level: 

  • Fundamentals of economics And Management
  • Fundamentals of Accounting
  • Fundamentals of Laws and Ethics
  • Fundamentals of Business Mathematics and Statistics

 

Intermediate Course:

Group I

  • Financial Accounting
  • Laws and Ethics
  • Direct Taxation
  • Cost Accounting

Group II

  • Operations Management and Strategic Management
  • Cost and Management Accounting and Financial Management
  • Indirect Taxation
  • Company Accounts and Audit

 

Final Course:

Group I –

  • Corporate Laws and Compliance
  • Strategic Financial Management
  • Strategic Cost Management-Decision Making
  • Direct Tax Laws and International Taxation

           Group II-

  • Corporate Financial Reporting
  • Indirect Tax Laws and Practice
  • Cost and Management Audit
  • Strategic Performance Management and Business valuation

This is a complete syllabus of the Certified Management Accountant course. Well, this course is one of the most important courses after 12th for commerce.
