Is this going to be the year in which you quit the rat race? Or at least choose your own route for it?

People are job-hopping more than ever. Platforms like Fiverr make the gig economy stronger than ever.

Plus, ever since we’ve all started working from home, we discovered that it’s actually quite cool to manage yourself, your time, and your projects—without someone physically looking over your shoulder.

Unsurprisingly, a lot more people are finally discovering their entrepreneurial side, either by starting side-projects, or even by just taking the dive without any floaties like a permanent contract at a job they secretly hate. 

Are you also ready to take that plunge, but not sure yet in what direction to jump? You don’t have to reinvent the wheel. 

Of course, you could just take parts of your current job and start doing that independently—like many social media managers turned freelance copywriters do. But maybe you’re looking to do a complete 180, and want a job that fits the times we live in, matches with your passions, and doesn’t feel like a job at all, actually. 

We’re not going to tell you how virtual assistants are in great demand and that dog sitting is fun. Instead, we’ve gathered 12 creative and fun ideas to inspire your entrepreneurial adventure. Ready to go?

 

1. Become a crypto coach

Are you one of those people who somehow understand cryptocurrencies—and maybe even NFTs—and have you been successfully investing in all the right coins? That’s a great way to make an (extra) income.

But what if you could do more with all that knowledge? People are reading up about it, but at the end of the day, it’s a lot more comforting when someone actually tells you how and when to buy. 

Let’s say, you know all about how to buy BTC on Moonpay, or any other type of bitcoin.

You could become a one-on-one coach about this, or even create an online course, to make this side-hustle all the more scalable.

2. Start an at-home cooking service

Do your friends and family often come over to devour your food? Are you a master at meal prepping and making something amazing from the weirdest leftovers someone has in their fridge?

Then you’re lucky, first of all. Many people struggle to come up with a pasta dish that isn’t the same as yesterday’s (and the ten days before) if they even have time to cook at all.

Now, you might not want to become a professional chef and spend most of your nights and weekends in a kitchen.

You could consider starting a delivery service, but that would mean you’d spend more time driving around than cooking—and you want to do what you love.

So if you’re a talented cook, consider starting a local business where you go to other people’s houses to meal prep.

You can opt to do the groceries and create meals for the entire week in their kitchen—which also saves you your own kitchen and sanity. 

While this is a little of an upscale type of service, there are surely plenty of people around who are tired of the same old take-away every day and would love to try your fresh food!

 

3. Make yourself a hyper-specialized social media expert

Right, social media marketing isn’t necessarily the most original job. But the big boys aren’t looking for generalists anymore, anyway.

Their Instagram and LinkedIn are up and running, they just want to turn it into a success. So if you know how to use any platform like no one else, offer that as a service. 

For instance, offer engagement services for Instagram. Or help people write great LinkedIn pages.

The more specific it gets, the easier it’ll become to sell to people who already know what they’re doing—but acknowledge they need your help.

 

4. Become a remote work trainer

If you’ve been a digital nomad since before 2020, or you adapted incredibly easily, you’ve got a lot of valuable skills to teach others.

A lot of people who are used to cubicles or trendy open-plan offices are still struggling with managing remote work. Which means their employers are also struggling with that.

You can sell yourself to organizations as a remote work coach who will help them get the most out of their current employees, teaching them healthy ways to manage their work-life balance and boost their productivity. 

 

5. Sell yourself as an ergonomic expert

Continuing on the previous point: a lot of people think they’ve got their home office routine completely under control. Meanwhile, they sound like a firecracker every time they get up for another cup of coffee. But no, they’re fine. 

If you’ve been working as a physiotherapist or in any other related field, and know a thing or two about working in a healthy position, market yourself to people who are working from home and don’t want to break their backs in the process.

With the right value proposition and laser-targeted market, plus maybe some referral deals and affiliate marketing with furniture stores, this could be a rather profitable business—and you’ll do a little good for others as well.

6. Finally start that food truck!

No, this is not the time to recommend anyone to open a restaurant, we’re well aware. But if it’s always been a dream of yours, and you don’t like to be at the same place all the time anyway, why not open a food truck?

Admittedly, this is not a new concept, but it’s still a booming business, and perfectly fits the time we live in of outdoor events and being flexible. You could even use it to travel a little further and further because your market will simply be wherever you go.  

 

7. Travel planner for digital nomads

Oddly specific, right? That’s just how we like it. 

Ever since remote work is becoming more accepted and standardized, people are dipping their toes into actual travel and work, instead of working from home. 

But if you think choosing a holiday destination is difficult, wait until you have to find a destination that has fast enough Wi-Fi to zoom your boss, has plenty of coworking spaces or at least coffee places that won’t kick you out when you type too loud.

A lot of digital nomads have been there, done that, and if that’s you, consider helping people find their way in this world. 

You could create guides on specific places and sell them, or make it all the more personal (and valuable!) by becoming a personal travel planner. You could even offer this service to companies who are encouraging their employees to use this time to explore the world while still getting the job done, for even more impact. 

 

 

8. Become that specialized tour guide in your own city

Live in a pretty place and know all about it? Now that tourism is starting to come back, you could use this coincidence to make a little money—while also getting out of the house.

Of course, there are plenty of standard businesses you could join to become a generic tour guide, pointing at the landmarks nobody was going to miss anyway. But with your specific, personal touch to it, you could make it into a little business too.

For instance, maybe you know all the best wine bars in your city, and you can create a tour around that. Or you were an enthusiastic dater in your early twenties and can show couples the most romantic spots in town. 

Make it quirky, and make it specific. You could create tours especially for the elderly, or for families with kids. Market yourself in relevant places and start walking!

 

9. Sell your photos as a stock photographer

Have you always loved photography, and do you have countless pictures that are great—but not really useful for you? Sell them as stock photos! 

Now, this will probably start out as a small side gig, but it’s nonetheless a lot of fun and gives you some reward for your creative work. You can upload your work on websites like Unsplash or Pexels and start earning money every time someone uses your photo. If things are looking up, you can even become more independent and start your own website.

With the amount of content that is being created every, well, minute, there’s a huge demand for stock footage, and anyone who frequently has to find that knows the most-used pictures by heart—so give them a fresh set of pictures!

Dream big, but do bigger

As you can see, there’s a wide variety of options out there if you want to start a business. No matter how niche your passion is, you can turn it into a little more than that, even if it would just be to make a passive income from selling courses about your knitting obsession. 

If you’re looking to double down on yourself and the opportunities out there nowadays, you’ll probably like this podcast on developing yourself by seizing opportunities when they arrive, too.

Gen Z makes up 20.35% of the U.S. population, and they’re the most diverse generation in terms of race and ethnicity. Known as the smartphone generation, they’re tech-savvy, avid social media users, and protective of the environment.

These are just some of the facts you should know about Gen Z to be able to understand them and get them excited about your brand. Consulting with a branding agency might be a good idea to attract the post-millennial generation, but getting informed first is a must. In this guide, we’ll explain six steps you should take to get Gen Z excited about your brand.

 

1. Appeal To Gen Z By Showing That You Care

 

Gen Z is a caring generation, with the likes of Greta Tunberg being their voice for nature preservation, and ultimately, saving the planet. Marketing to these teens and adolescents shouldn’t be flashy or flamboyant—they prefer eco-friendly and ethical brands.

Treat the planet and your employees with respect and gratitude if you want to grab the attention of Gen Z. Your brand policy should be in line with these attitudes if you want to successfully grab Gen Z’s attention.

 

2. Have A Healthy Purpose

 

Most businesses focus solely on the numbers and the income they make. That might be fine with some of the older generations, but the Gen Z population isn’t so product or money-oriented. They prefer a brand that has a clear purpose and tries to make the world a better place.

If you show initiative to help your community, spread positivity, preserve nature, fight for those less fortunate, and stand up to social injustice, you’ll look good in the eyes of everyone, especially younger generations.

 

3. Interact With Them On Multiple Platforms

 

Gen Z is a very interactive generation, they are native to social media platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, Tik Tok, YouTube, Twitter, and more. Responding to your audience through these outlets can increase their interest and gain their loyalty.

Younger audiences are brand conscious and gladly flock to brands that are listening and catering to their needs. Loyalty is a two-way street, and you have to build it gradually but remember to maintain it throughout your brand expansion and marketing content for future success.

 

4. Don’t Patronize Or Lie To Gen Z

 

Being honest is not always easy, but if you want your brand to succeed, you’ll have to be genuine and inspiring. False advertising doesn’t sit well with anyone, but the older generation tends to succumb to it more than the younger ones.

Try to be relatable and personalize your communication with the Gen Z audience. It will go a long way if you treat them with respect and be upfront and honest.

 

5. Focus On Mobile Users

 

Baby boomers might have trouble adjusting to the tech era, but Gen Z is born in it. The majority of this population uses smartphones and tablets as their go-to digital devices. Many brands prioritize the desktop experience, but that shouldn’t be the case anymore.

If you want to target younger users, make sure the mobile experience is smooth, fast, and optimal. Invest the same or higher amounts in the mobile version of your website — don’t necessarily prioritize the desktop experience.

For example, you can hire a Chicago web design company to help you out with this, if you’re living in Chicago. There are companies that specialize in this all over the US, and employing industry-leading experts can surely help your brand.

 

6. Cut Straight to the Point—They Have Shorter Attention Spans!

 

The digital era brought many benefits to brands across all industries. However, it also brought some flaws to the younger generations — their attention spans are very short, around eight seconds, which is four seconds shorter than the attentions of millennials.

How do you grab someone’s attention in such a short period of time? There are a few schools of thought on this.

First, you need to be creative and employ modern media methods whether using Facebook ads or Instagram feeds to reach your audience. Most people are visual learners and prefer this medium, which also applies to Gen Z.

Second, you can try to offer an incentive for them to sign up for an email list of some sort.  While they’re typically weary of doing this, if you offer the right lead magnet it’s possible to get them signed up.  From there, use an email marketing platform from this list to deliver a ton of value over the course of a few weeks and then focus on your “ask”.

Generally speaking, Gen Z likes extremely short videos, such as those featured in Instagram Reels or on Tik Tok. Adding these catchy videos to your website, within emails, or even better, reaching out to audiences on these dominant social media platforms, can be the key to grabbing their attention.

If you’re not familiar with using these apps, consider hiring Gen Z influencers to help you out. They can tag and showcase your brand, which is more likely to catch the attention of Gen Z than relying solely on your main website.

 

Parting Advice

 

Remember that some of these tips will work better than the others because Gen Z is a vast population with over 67 million people in the US alone. If you’re considerate and put in the effort to personalize your brand toward Gen Z, you’ll likely get their attention.

When it comes to websites, one of the most important choices you can make is regarding a web hosting plan. In fact, a web hosting plan is the backbone of your website, no matter what business you have.

Not only does it affect your website’s performance, but it also impacts your business’s success. And because there are so many web hosting options out there, picking the right one might seem overwhelming at first.

However, the key to selecting the right web hosting plan is understanding your website and business requirements. For instance, picking a cheaper hosting plan can slow down your website, depending on the amount of traffic it gets. And since every business must have an online presence nowadays, you have to make sure that the web hosting plan fits your needs perfectly.

 

Web Hosting Types

First of all, before you make a choice, you will need to understand the basic types of web hosting. And while they might seem quite similar at first, each has its pros and cons.

 

Shared Hosting

Shared hosting is a service where a number of websites reside on one web server connected to the internet. That works by splitting the server into thousands of smaller accounts, each sharing the server’s bandwidth and storage.

Generally speaking, shared hosting is the budget option, as the entry cost is much lower compared to other types of hosting. But as a result, there are some security concerns, such as DDoS attacks, which make the sharing of server resources a potential risk factor. You should consider shared hosting for:

  • Static websites
  • Startup or local businesses
  • Low traffic websites
  • Smaller budgets

 

VPS Hosting

VPS hosting uses virtualization to replicate a dedicated environment within a shared server. It works by creating multiple virtual instances, each with its own resources and limitations. But overall, all VPS instances are granted a portion of the parent server’s hardware, similar to shared hosting.

However, compared to shared hosting, VPS offers better performance and more freedom. It also allows for deeper customization and control over your server and website. As a result, VPS hosting is perfect for:

  • High traffic websites
  • E-commerce websites
  • Medium to big-budget businesses

 

Dedicated Hosting

Dedicated hosting permits you to rent an entire server, giving you access to all its resources. And since dedicated servers are isolated from one another, users get full access to configure their server any way they want. As such, dedicated hosting provides the highest level of resource allocation, privacy, and control.

It’s important to note that dedicated servers are more suited for big corporations that require high uptimes and good speeds. That’s because dedicated servers are much more costly than any other similar hosting plan. But overall, dedicated hosting works best for:

  • High budget businesses
  • E-commerce websites
  • High traffic websites
  • Group of websites

 

How Can I Choose the Right Web Hosting Plan?

As previously mentioned, choosing the right web hosting plan boils down to knowledge. Because of that, we recommend you ask yourself the following questions before proceeding with any web hosting out there.

 

1. How Much Am I Willing To Invest In Web Hosting?

The first thing you must do is to determine a budget for web hosting. If you’re on a limited and strict budget, you can go for a cheaper option such as shared hosting. But if you can invest more, then you should consider VPS or even dedicated hosting.

However, while the upfront cost can seem affordable at first, you will need to think about renewal prices too. For example, a hosting company may allow you to sign up for as little as $5 but then ask for ten times that amount when renewing their service. And since switching web hosts can be hard, you will have no choice but to cave in to their demands.

 

2. How Much Traffic Am I Expecting?

The more web traffic your site receives, the more bandwidth it will require to keep running smoothly. As a result, you need to estimate the number of monthly visitors before you decide on a web hosting service. But predicting the right amount of traffic is hard, if not impossible.

On the one hand, you can choose a high bandwidth plan, paying for more than you could ever use. On the other hand, restricted bandwidth will result in a bad user experience if your website gets too popular. The best solution is to choose a web hosting plan that can scale on demand.

Our advice is to go for a VPS plan since it’s only a bit more expensive than shared hosting. Also, it offers more bandwidth and the option of increasing it depending on the growth of your website.

 

3. What Type Of Content Do I Plan To Use?

Any server has a limited number of resources, including bandwidth and, more importantly, space. As such, you should think about what type of content you plan to upload on the server. For instance, video content requires much more space and bandwidth than image or text content.

Additionally, some web hosting providers can up the costs once you go over a threshold. In fact, hosting providers that supposedly offer unlimited space will either make you pay more or close your website altogether once you use too many resources. That’s because you are actually sharing the disk space with other users, meaning that the more space you need, the less the others will have.

As a result, you should always read the terms of service before you try any type of hosting, especially unlimited ones.

 

4. Where Are The Web Hosting Servers Located?

Nowadays, privacy is not only an option, it’s a must — especially if your website handles sensitive information. And when it comes to data privacy and security, server location is extremely important.

Not only do you want your server to be in a secure location, but you want it to provide additional layers of defense in case of cyberattacks. And while most hosting servers should be secure, some offer better protection and security features.

Additionally, some countries block access to specific types of content. In other words, if the server is located in a country where your content is forbidden, the website will be taken down. As a result, you will want to carefully choose the location of your server depending on what type of content you want to upload.

If you want to avoid all that trouble, your best bet is to go for offshore hosting. That’s because it offers total data privacy, anonymity, and most importantly, a flexible content policy.

 

5. What Are The Server Uptime Records?

It doesn’t really matter what kind of website you own — uptime is important. Needless to say, you want a reliable host that can guarantee almost 100% uptime. However, some hosts define uptime differently, so you should look up the company’s policy before signing up.

And since downtime is almost inevitable, you must know how the hosting provider handles it. You need to understand that downtime is as much a technical issue as a PR issue. How a company responds to a downtime crisis says a lot about its commitment to customer satisfaction.

Our advice is to look for reviews that indicate the reliability and history of the web hosting service. For instance, European hosting providers are well-known for their high uptime. That is due to modern infrastructure and cutting-edge technology, which often surpasses that of the US.

 

6. Which Level Of Customization Do I Need?

Depending on what kind of website you want, customization is a key factor in deciding on a web hosting plan. If you plan to use custom apps or specialized software, you will need a comprehensive plan that gives you complete control. And as we previously brushed on, each type of hosting solution allows for different levels of customization.

Shared hosting is restrictive since any change is applied to all the websites operating on that server. However, VPS hosting allows users to customize their environment without disrupting others. And if you want full customization, you should always go for dedicated hosting, since it provides you with full control over the server.

 

7. Will I Be Collecting Sensitive Information?

If you are handling customer data, encryption and backups should be your highest priority. And while every hosting company provides a certain level of security, some are better at withstanding cyber-attacks than others.

Shared hosting comes short when talking about security since multiple websites share a single server. So one flaw can cripple all websites hosted on that particular server, leaving you vulnerable to potential attacks. That can be devastating if you are saving customer information such as passwords or credit card details.

On the opposite spectrum, dedicated servers offer the highest level of security. As a result, all of the resources go into protecting your website and your customer’s data. That’s exactly why most e-commerce websites go for dedicated servers instead of other cheaper options.

 

The Bottom Line

All in all, choosing the perfect web hosting plan depends entirely on your goal and budget. However, it’s important to understand that the success of your website is tied to your web host. As an example, web hosting can affect the SEO ranking of your website, reducing or increasing its popularity. Besides that, web hosting reliability can mean the difference between a simple visitor and a new customer.

Overall, we recommend that you pay close attention to all the features we previously discussed when picking a host. You should also research different companies and hosting plans before committing to any of them. But you won’t have anything to worry about as long as you follow our tips and pay attention to online reviews and feedback.

Aside from creating the most innovative products and services, entrepreneurs should also prioritize advertising their business. Advertising is vital in the success of every business as this can generate brand loyalty, increase company traffic, and attract new customers.

Although important, only a very few entrepreneurs know how to properly and effectively advertise their businesses. More often than not, entrepreneurs would implement advertising strategies that don’t work or meet their expectations, resulting in wasted resources.

If you’re looking for ways to advertise your business better, take note of the following:

1. Entrust Professionals

Advertising your business can be tough, especially if you don’t have any existing campaign in place. For small and start-up businesses, creating their own advertising campaign and implementing it properly can be very overwhelming as they won’t have any idea how to start.

2. Use Social Media

Billions of people use social media every day, which is why businesses should utilize this platform to advertise their businesses. Thus, regardless of who your target audience is, it’ll be very easy for you to reach them through social media.

Using social media is one of the best ways to advertise your business. Aside from being accessible to billions of people worldwide, social media is also inexpensive and easy to navigate.

Some of the most common ways to advertise on social media are:

  • Adding branded hashtags to the platform;
  • Creating an ambassador or loyalty program;
  • Hosting contests and giveaways;
  • Tagging products in your posts; and
  • Use branded or unique social icons on your profiles.

Even if you can do a lot of things on social media, remember to only choose one or two platforms that suit the nature of your business. Creating profiles on all social media platforms can result in having a stale or outdated profile, which can shoo away potential customers.

 

3. Get A Website

Because of the advent of technology, customers are now relying on the World Wide Web for their buying decisions. Before a person pays for a new product or service, they’ll do a search online to know more about the legitimacy of the business and the effectiveness of their offerings. Customers will likely choose to do buy from a business that’s visible on various online platforms.

Aside from maximizing social media, creating a website is also an effective way of advertising your business. Contrary to popular belief, having a website is a requirement among businesses and is no longer a luxury or accessory to their marketing efforts.

An effective business website can become your greatest advertising tool and an avenue for engaging with customers. A good website also allows your business to sell products and services and address concerns left by online users.

 

4. Get Online Customer Reviews

As mentioned, the identity your business creates online can influence a customer’s decision to buy from you. It’ll be challenging for you to attract customers if they don’t have any idea how your products work or know that your business actually exists. If you don’t want any of these to happen, encourage your customers to leave reviews.

Getting online reviews from your customers is an effective and expensive way of advertising your business. The testimonials left by your previous customers will give potential customers an idea of how your business operates and what products and services can they get from you. Ideally, you should encourage customers to leave reviews on your website and various social media platforms.

If customers left negative reviews on your website, don’t fret because there are many ways for your business to use these to your advantage. Instead of merely ignoring negative comments online, reply to these promptly, apologize, and offer solutions. Doing this will make the customer who left the comment feel valued and heard. Taking the time to respond to negative comments online can also attract new customers as this will show how your business values the opinion of all of your customers.

 

5. Turn Your Vehicle Into A Mobile Billboard

Billboards have been used by countless businesses all over the world when advertising their products and services, which means that using the same won’t make your business unique. This is especially true if the billboard you’re planning to lease is situated in an area with billboards from established and well-known businesses.

If you’re looking for unique ways of advertising your business, turn your vehicle into a mobile billboard. This advertising is similar to traditional billboards but can provide better outcomes because it can reach more audiences.

You can turn your vehicle into a mobile billboard by applying removable stickers of your logo, company name, and contact details. Using a mobile billboard is a creative way of advertising your business that’ll surely gain the attention of the people who’ll see your vehicle.

 

6. Utilize Articles And Blogs

If you’ve been operating in the business arena for years, you’d likely heard the saying, ‘content is king.’ This remains true up until this very day because the content is vital for advertising any type of business.

Another way to advertise your business better is by creating high-quality articles and blogs. Regularly creating and posting high-quality articles and blogs about your business can improve your brand reputation and your SEO efforts, bringing in new leads and showcasing your expertise in certain niches or industries.

If you’re having a hard time what to write about, determine what your business’s products and services are, then start from there. For example, if your business offers web designing services, write high-quality articles and blogs that assess website usability. If your business sells real estate properties, talk about how sellers can prepare their properties for an open house.

 

Remember To Audit

Regardless of how you’re planning to advertise your business, always allot resources to regularly audit your efforts. Auditing is vital when implementing advertising strategies as this will enable you to determine which works and which doesn’t. It’ll also pinpoint which advertising strategies should you spend your business’s resources on.
